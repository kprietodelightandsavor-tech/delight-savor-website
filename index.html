The Heart of It

Literature and language arts are the table — the place where ideas are served and savored, where students learn not just to read words, but to live with them. Charlotte Mason believed that children are not vessels to fill, but persons to engage. A living book doesn't just convey information; it initiates a relationship between the reader and an idea. Our curriculum is built on that conviction. We narrate, we annotate, we linger. We bring great texts into conversation with the Great Conversation — Augustine, Aristotle, Rousseau — and we trust students to hold the weight of real ideas.

— The Delight & Savor Approach

Meet the Maker

Hello, I'm Kim.

I spent nine years as a public school English teacher — AP Language & Composition, AP British Literature, all of it — before I brought my own children home and discovered that teaching looks completely different when you're doing it around a kitchen table on a Texas ranch with alpacas wandering past the window.

Delight & Savor was born from the conviction that upper-level literature doesn't have to be sterile, test-driven, or disconnected from real life. It can be nourishing. It can be a feast. I hold a master's in Literary Studies, and I teach a weekly co-op class for high school students using the same materials I sell here — so what you're getting has been field-tested in a real classroom, by real students, with a real teacher.
