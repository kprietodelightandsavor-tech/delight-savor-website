Tragedy & Gothic

Macbeth

William Shakespeare

A 15-week literature companion built around Charlotte Mason narration, the DELIGHT analytical framework, and Augustine's theology of ordered and disordered love. Students read the play closely, narrate weekly, and work toward a full thesis argument by the final weeks. The Honors Track pushes deeper into Shakespeare's source material and the nature of conscience.

Which Version Is Right for You?

Co-op vs. Home Study

Every unit comes in two versions — one designed for a weekly co-op classroom, one for the independent home student. The content and rigor are the same; the structure and pacing differ.

🏛️

Co-op Version

For weekly classroom use · $18

  • Designed for 1.5-hour weekly sessions
  • Includes full teacher's guide with discussion prompts, timing notes, and gray-box teacher annotations
  • Student handouts formatted for in-class narration and discussion
  • Absent student guides so no one falls behind
  • Oral narration as the backbone — writing builds gradually across the semester
  • Honors Track extensions for students ready to go deeper
📖

Home Study Version

For independent learners · $15

  • Flexible pacing — complete each week's work in 2–3 sessions
  • Student-facing handouts designed for independent reading and narration
  • Parent/teacher guide with light facilitation notes
  • Written narration options included for every week
  • Same DELIGHT framework and thesis progression as co-op version
  • Family license — covers all students in one household

Available in Both Versions

The Honors Track

Each unit includes optional Honors Track extensions — deeper reading, additional secondary sources, and more demanding writing prompts — for the student who is ready to be genuinely challenged. The Honors Track is woven into the same handouts; no separate purchase needed.
