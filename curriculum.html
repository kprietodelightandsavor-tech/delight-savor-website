In the Margin

A student companion app that lives alongside the course — on any device, no download required.

  • Weekly assignment tracker with handout links
  • Reading Companion (chapters, characters, themes, key passages)
  • Writing Table — guided 7-step composition tool
  • Narration Coach (Find It · Follow It · Frame It)
  • Literary Devices reference
  • Commonplace Journal (Consider the Lilies)

Students save it to their home screen like an app. Works on phone, tablet, or laptop — wherever they read.

The Art of Tragedy

Two of the most powerful works in the Western canon — one a study in ambition and moral collapse, the other in disordered love and its consequences. Series 1 asks students to read slowly, narrate faithfully, and begin to write with real argument.

Series 1 · Fall

Macbeth

William Shakespeare

A 15-week literature companion built around Charlotte Mason narration, the DELIGHT analytical framework, and Augustine’s theology of ordered and disordered love. Students read the play closely, narrate weekly, and work toward a full thesis argument by the final weeks. The Honors Track pushes deeper into Shakespeare’s source material and the nature of conscience.

Wuthering Heights

Emily Brontë

A 15-week deep dive into Brontë’s gothic masterpiece, examining coercive control, Rousseau’s natural man, and the Augustinian framework of love’s disorder. Students practice oral narration, build toward written argument, and engage with a curated selection of secondary texts. One of the most intellectually rich units in the series.
